Effective tax rates paid by 50 largest companies in S&P 500

The year has ended and tax season is right around the corner (January at time of writing this). So, I thought what better way to mark the occasion than to do a quick pull to see what the largest public companies are paying in taxes. I did a quick scrape of tax rates on Reuters for the 50 largest companies by market cap in the S&P 500, pulling both the 5 year average and trailing 12 month tax rates.

After pulling the data, I first split the tax rates out by sector, as shown below:




Here, we can see that on average over the past 5 years (blue bar) Healthcare and IT pay the least taxes, while Energy normally pays the most. However, since the price of oil kind of crashed, Energy companies are now (unsurprisingly) bottoming out the list for the trailing 12 month tax rate.
